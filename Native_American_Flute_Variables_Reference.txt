NATIVE AMERICAN FLUTE CALCULATION VARIABLES REFERENCE
=====================================================

This document provides a comprehensive reference for all variables used in Native American flute calculations, based on acoustic principles and design methodologies from Prairie (2006), Shands (2010), and other authoritative sources.

=====================================================
1. ACOUSTIC VARIABLES
=====================================================

FUNDAMENTAL FREQUENCY (f0)
- Definition: The lowest resonant frequency of the air column
- Units: Hertz (Hz)
- Calculation: f0 = c / (4 * Leff)
- Where: c = speed of sound (343 m/s at 20°C), Leff = effective length
- Implementation: Determines the key/scale of the flute
- Measurement: Use frequency analyzer or tuner

SPEED OF SOUND (c)
- Definition: Velocity of sound waves in air
- Units: meters per second (m/s)
- Value: 343 m/s at 20°C, varies with temperature
- Calculation: c = 331.3 + (0.606 * T) where T = temperature in °C
- Implementation: Used in all frequency calculations
- Measurement: Calculated from ambient temperature

EFFECTIVE LENGTH (Leff)
- Definition: The acoustically effective length of the air column
- Units: meters (m)
- Calculation: Leff = L + ΔL (physical length + end corrections)
- Implementation: Determines fundamental frequency
- Measurement: Physical measurement plus calculated end corrections

=====================================================
2. GEOMETRIC VARIABLES
=====================================================

BORE LENGTH (L)
- Definition: Physical length of the main air column from mouthpiece to open end
- Units: meters (m) or inches
- Typical range: 0.3-0.8m (12-32 inches)
- Implementation: Primary determinant of fundamental frequency
- Measurement: Direct measurement with ruler or calipers

BORE DIAMETER (D)
- Definition: Internal diameter of the main air column
- Units: meters (m) or inches
- Typical range: 0.015-0.025m (0.6-1.0 inches)
- Implementation: Affects timbre, volume, and end corrections
- Measurement: Internal calipers or bore gauge

WALL THICKNESS (t)
- Definition: Thickness of the flute wall material
- Units: meters (m) or inches
- Typical range: 0.003-0.008m (0.12-0.32 inches)
- Implementation: Affects structural integrity and some acoustic properties
- Measurement: Calipers on cross-section

=====================================================
3. TUNING VARIABLES
=====================================================

K1 (First End Correction Factor)
- Definition: End correction for the mouthpiece/block end/north end
- Units: dimensionless (typically 0.3-0.6)
- Calculation: K1 = 0.3 + 0.2 * (D/L)^0.5
- Implementation: Added to physical length for effective length calculation
- Measurement: Derived from bore geometry

K2 (Second End Correction Factor)
- Definition: End correction for the open end/south end
- Units: dimensionless (typically 0.6-0.8)
- Calculation: K2 = 0.6 + 0.1 * (D/L)^0.5
- Implementation: Added to physical length for effective length calculation
- Measurement: Derived from bore geometry

END CORRECTION (ΔL)
- Definition: Total end correction for both ends
- Units: meters (m)
- Calculation: ΔL = K1 * D + K2 * D
- Implementation: Compensates for acoustic behavior at open ends
- Measurement: Calculated from K1, K2, and bore diameter

=====================================================
4. AIR COLUMN VARIABLES
=====================================================

SLOW AIR CHAMBER LENGTH (L_sac)
- Definition: Length of the slow air chamber from mouthpiece to block
- Units: meters (m)
- Typical range: 0.05-0.15m (2-6 inches)
- Implementation: Affects breath control and initial air flow
- Measurement: Direct measurement from mouthpiece to block

SLOW AIR CHAMBER DIAMETER (D_sac)
- Definition: Diameter of the slow air chamber
- Units: meters (m)
- Typically same as bore diameter
- Implementation: Affects air flow characteristics
- Measurement: Internal calipers

BLOCK HEIGHT (h_block)
- Definition: Height of the block above the sound hole
- Units: meters (m)
- Typical range: 0.003-0.008m (0.12-0.32 inches)
- Implementation: Critical for proper air splitting and sound production
- Measurement: Calipers or depth gauge

SOUND HOLE WIDTH (w_sound)
- Definition: Width of the sound hole (TSH - True Sound Hole)
- Units: meters (m)
- Typical range: 0.008-0.015m (0.32-0.6 inches)
- Implementation: Affects air splitting and sound quality
- Measurement: Calipers across the sound hole

SOUND HOLE LENGTH (l_sound)
- Definition: Length of the sound hole
- Units: meters (m)
- Typical range: 0.015-0.025m (0.6-1.0 inches)
- Implementation: Works with width to determine air splitting characteristics
- Measurement: Calipers along the sound hole

=====================================================
5. TONE HOLE VARIABLES
=====================================================

TONE HOLE DIAMETER (d_th)
- Definition: Diameter of individual tone holes
- Units: meters (m)
- Typical range: 0.006-0.012m (0.24-0.48 inches)
- Implementation: Affects pitch and volume of individual notes
- Measurement: Calipers or hole gauge

TONE HOLE POSITION (x_th)
- Definition: Distance from mouthpiece to center of tone hole
- Units: meters (m)
- Calculation: Based on desired frequency and acoustic principles
- Implementation: Determines the pitch of each note
- Measurement: Ruler from mouthpiece to hole center

TONE HOLE SPACING (s_th)
- Definition: Distance between adjacent tone holes
- Units: meters (m)
- Calculation: x_th(n+1) - x_th(n)
- Implementation: Affects playability and finger reach
- Measurement: Ruler between hole centers

TONE HOLE DEPTH (h_th)
- Definition: Depth of tone hole drilling
- Units: meters (m)
- Typically: 0.5 * wall thickness
- Implementation: Affects tone hole end correction
- Measurement: Depth gauge or calipers

=====================================================
6. END CORRECTION VARIABLES
=====================================================

MOUTHPIECE END CORRECTION (ΔL_mp)
- Definition: End correction for the mouthpiece end
- Units: meters (m)
- Calculation: ΔL_mp = K1 * D
- Implementation: Compensates for acoustic behavior at mouthpiece
- Measurement: Calculated from K1 and bore diameter

OPEN END CORRECTION (ΔL_open)
- Definition: End correction for the open end
- Units: meters (m)
- Calculation: ΔL_open = K2 * D
- Implementation: Compensates for acoustic behavior at open end
- Measurement: Calculated from K2 and bore diameter

TONE HOLE END CORRECTION (ΔL_th)
- Definition: End correction for open tone holes
- Units: meters (m)
- Calculation: ΔL_th = 0.6 * d_th
- Implementation: Affects effective length when holes are open
- Measurement: Calculated from tone hole diameter

=====================================================
7. CALCULATION CONSTANTS
=====================================================

ACOUSTIC IMPEDANCE (Z)
- Definition: Ratio of pressure to volume velocity
- Units: Pa·s/m³
- Calculation: Z = ρc/A where ρ = air density, c = speed of sound, A = cross-sectional area
- Implementation: Used in advanced acoustic modeling
- Measurement: Calculated from air properties and geometry

AIR DENSITY (ρ)
- Definition: Mass per unit volume of air
- Units: kg/m³
- Value: 1.204 kg/m³ at 20°C, 1 atm
- Calculation: ρ = 1.293 * (273.15 / (273.15 + T)) * (P / 101325)
- Implementation: Used in impedance calculations
- Measurement: Calculated from temperature and pressure

CROSS-SECTIONAL AREA (A)
- Definition: Area of the bore cross-section
- Units: square meters (m²)
- Calculation: A = π * (D/2)²
- Implementation: Used in impedance and flow calculations
- Measurement: Calculated from bore diameter

=====================================================
8. MEASUREMENT TECHNIQUES
=====================================================

PHYSICAL MEASUREMENTS
- Use precision calipers for diameters and thicknesses
- Use rulers or measuring tapes for lengths
- Use depth gauges for hole depths
- Record measurements to nearest 0.1mm (0.004 inches)

ACOUSTIC MEASUREMENTS
- Use digital tuner or frequency analyzer for pitch
- Measure at consistent temperature (20°C recommended)
- Use consistent blowing pressure
- Record frequencies to nearest 1 Hz

CALCULATION VERIFICATION
- Compare calculated frequencies with measured frequencies
- Adjust end correction factors if needed
- Verify tone hole positions produce correct intervals
- Test playability and intonation

=====================================================
COMMON CALCULATION FORMULAS
=====================================================

FUNDAMENTAL FREQUENCY:
f0 = c / (4 * Leff)

EFFECTIVE LENGTH:
Leff = L + K1*D + K2*D

TONE HOLE POSITION (approximate):
x_th = (c / (4 * f_desired)) - (K1 * D) - (0.6 * d_th)

END CORRECTION FACTORS:
K1 = 0.3 + 0.2 * (D/L)^0.5
K2 = 0.6 + 0.1 * (D/L)^0.5

TEMPERATURE CORRECTION:
c = 331.3 + (0.606 * T)

=====================================================
DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS
=====================================================

SCALE AND KEY
- Choose fundamental frequency based on desired key
- Consider player's hand size for tone hole spacing
- Balance between acoustic theory and practical playability

MATERIAL PROPERTIES
- Wood density affects resonance characteristics
- Wall thickness affects structural integrity
- Finish affects air flow and moisture resistance

ACOUSTIC OPTIMIZATION
- Minimize end corrections for better intonation
- Optimize tone hole sizes for volume and clarity
- Balance bore diameter for timbre and playability
