DESIGN RECOMMENDATIONS:
=======================

FOR LOWER NOTES (A4 to A#4):
- Use 30mm inner diameter
- Use longer bore (150-160mm)
- Smaller slow air chamber (30-40mm)

FOR HIGHER NOTES (B4 to C5):
- Use 20mm inner diameter  
- Use shorter bore (140-150mm)
- Larger slow air chamber (40-50mm)

ACOUSTIC PRINCIPLES:
====================

The fundamental frequency is determined by:
f0 = c / (4 * Leff)

Where:
- c = speed of sound (343 m/s)
- Leff = effective length = physical length + end corrections

End corrections depend on:
- Bore diameter (D)
- Bore length (L)
- K1 = 0.3 + 0.2 * sqrt(D/L)  (mouthpiece end)
- K2 = 0.6 + 0.1 * sqrt(D/L)  (open end)

Larger diameter → larger end corrections → longer effective length → lower pitch
Longer bore → longer effective length → lower pitch

PRACTICAL CONSIDERATIONS:
=========================

1. Slow Air Chamber Size:
   - Minimum recommended: ~30mm (for proper air flow)
   - Maximum practical: ~50mm (to maximize bore length)

2. Block Area:
   - Typically requires ~10mm for the fipple mechanism
   - Includes sound hole, block, and air channel

3. Total Length Allocation:
   - Slow Air Chamber: 30-50mm
   - Block Area: ~10mm
   - Main Bore: 140-160mm
   - Total: 200mm

4. Tuning Flexibility:
   - You can fine-tune by adjusting:
     * Slow air chamber length
     * Block position
     * Exact bore length
   - Small adjustments (±5mm) can shift pitch by ~20-30 Hz
